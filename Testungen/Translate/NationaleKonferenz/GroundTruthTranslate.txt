A warm welcome to the 13th National Maritime Conference, themed “Strengthening Our Region, Protecting the Climate, Shaping the Future.”
The days when ports, seafaring, and the maritime industry were acknowledged but not taken seriously are over; during the current crises, they have all performed exceptionally well. For that, I would like to say thank you today!
This is a great industry—from marine technology to shipbrokers to mechanics to all the other fields involved—and it’s one where I can actually do something good for the planet: namely, protect the climate. 
We need collective strength; we need a united, strong maritime community that tackles the challenges of the future together. One goal that unites us all: the operational readiness of the German Navy. After all, ultimately we all want the German Navy to defend our values and interests with modern and capable German vessels. The “German tempo”—which applies to shipbuilding—must also apply in the future to the German flag. That is what we want to carry forward; we must reduce bureaucracy here; we must become faster.
This is a new way of thinking that also justifies making certain decisions that go against the strict tenets of economics and then providing the necessary political and financial support to seize the opportunities that arise for propulsion systems like converter stations for special-purpose ships, and to rethink and build ports as part of this infrastructure. That, in essence, is the range of tasks we face in the coming years. We are in a global competitive environment, but we face this competition with broad chest and with the confidence that we will successfully prevail in this competition because you have been so successful in the past. Thank you very much!
